About Me

I'm a second year PhD student in Machine Learning at the Georgia Institute of Technology. I graduated in May 2018 with my Bachelors in Electrical Engineering from the University of Kansas with a focus in optimization and signal processing. I'm fortunate to be co-advised by Dr. Morris Cohen and Dr. Mark Davenport. I'm happy to annouce that I am currently supported by the NDSEG Fellowship.

My research is focused around applying a mix of canonical and modern time series and machine learning methods to forecasting problems. One example is the application of Long short-term memory networks to forecasting magnetic substorms. I am also interested in explainability in machine learning. In particular, i'm interested in drawing conclusions from ML results that motivate decision making by people who are otherwise unfamiliar with machine learning.

Aside from research, I engage in a number of extracurricular activities. I am involved in the musician's network on Georgia Tech's campus, and I play guitar in an indie rock/pop band. We have had the opportunity to play original music at different venues in Atlanta.
