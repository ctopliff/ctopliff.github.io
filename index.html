About Me

I'm a second year PhD student in Machine Learning at the Georgia Institute of Technology. I graduated in May 2018 with my Bachelors in Electrical Engineering from the University of Kansas with a focus in optimization and signal processing. I'm fortunate to be co-advised by Dr. Morris Cohen and Dr. Mark Davenport. My current research is focused on driving the state of the art in space weather forecasting using a mix of canonical time series methods as well as new machine learning methods, such as recurrent neural networks.

Aside from research, I engage in a number of extracurricular activities. I am heavily involved in the musician's network on Georgia Tech's campus, and I play guitar in an indie rock/pop band. We have had the opportunity to play original music at different venues in Atlanta.
